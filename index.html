Services

Ambience &
Background Music

Hotels, restaurants, cafes, bars, garden parties… you get the idea. Reggie can provide modest music which, like the soundtrack of a well-made film, will enhance the atmosphere and mood.

Weddings

Receptions, breakfasts and post-functions. All kinds of packages are available to make your special day that bit more special. If you have a specific piece or pieces of music in mind do make a request!

Private Hire

If you’re interested in organising a recital, or perhaps featuring a guest artist as part of a concert, Reggie has tremendous experience at presenting and performing to audiences. He has a large repertoire of concert music he can tailor towards the event.

Reggie can provide up to five hours worth of live music. Generally every hour will include a 10 minute break which can be occupied with pre-recorded music.

If suitable Reggie can provide his own amplification. Reggie uses an RC3 Condenser mic with a 600i Yamaha PA System; this equipment can easily fill an open area with 100+ people, as well as comfortably occupying a more intimate setting. Condenser microphones are traditionally used with orchestras; Reggie chooses to use this type of mic as it allows his guitar to be amplified without any discrepancy in tone production, giving an unblemished end product.

Rates are subjective to the event you’re organising; feel free to get in touch with Reggie for an estimate or more information.

Tuition

Private Tuition

Reggie offers private one on one tuition at his studio. He has great experience working with absolute beginners, as well as developed players who have been out of practice.


Teaching At Schools

Reggie teaches guitar and piano at Sidmouth Primary School. He finds working with children to be engaging as well as thoroughly rewarding. If you are a school and are interested in expanding the music services you offer to your students, then Reggie would be delighted to hear from you.

I find a great sense of fulfilment from teaching and working with people who have a positive attitude towards their own improvement. Having dedicated many years to guitar, and completing a masters in Music Performance, I believe I am fully qualified to pass on the knowledge I have learnt, and relearnt, about guitar and musicianship proficiently to aspiring players.

My mission statement is:

“As a teacher of guitar my job is to develop the abilities you need to realise what you would like to get out of music and guitar. Additionally I will introduce you to other ideas that I believe coincide with your interests for the benefit of your own development as a guitarist and a musician.”

I teach a variety of styles; generally what I advise my students is to first establish a proficient technique at guitar playing, then invest that technique into the kind of music that they’re interested in. I generate my own resources which means my students do not need to additionally purchase sheet music to learn from.

First sessions are free of charge; it will be mostly spent discussing what you want to get out of guitar and how I can help you to achieve that.

If you do not own a guitar I can happily supply you with one until you make the commitment.

My teaching rates are £12 per 30 minutes, £18 for 45 minutes and £24 for one hour.

I am DBS checked as of July 2015

Bio

Reggie Lawrence is a freelance performer and guitar teacher based in Hull. After five years of higher education, Reggie recently finished a masters in Music Performance at the University of Hull in addition to his undergraduate there.

Reggie was first introduced to the guitar when he was seven years old and has been submerged in the music world ever since. Outside of guitar Reggie plays the piano, theorbo and ukulele. His love of music does not stop at performing; he is an accomplished composer who has written music for the University of Hull, as well as the software development company Smashed Crab Studio.

Reggie has toured with several musicians including the guitarist Richard Silvester. Reggie belongs to the guitar trio Three Quarters with accomplished guitarists Matt Burton and Scott Williams. The trio perform nationally and are primarily interested in 21st century repertoire.

Reggie is also interested in editing music, of which the academic side of his masters was focused towards. Reggie uses his own editings in his music teaching which provide an accessible means for developing guitarists to interpret music.
